INDIAN COUNCIL OF MEDICAL RESEARCH (ICMR) DIETARY GUIDELINES FOR INDIANS (DIABETES FOCUS) - 2024 SUMMARY

1. MACRONUTRIENT DISTRIBUTION (DIABETIC PATIENTS)
   - Carbohydrates: 50–55% of total energy. (Should not be <130g/day for Type 1).
   - Protein: 10–15% of total energy. (Ideally 0.83g/kg body weight/day).
   - Fats: 20–30% of total energy. (Saturated fats <10%, Cholesterol <300mg/day).
   - Fiber: Recommended minimum 14g per 1000 kcal.

2. FOOD GROUP RECOMMENDATIONS
   - Cereals & Millets: Limit to <45% of total calories.
   - Pulses/Beans/Meat: Up to 15% of total calories.
   - Nuts/Vegetables/Fruits/Milk: Balance of remaining calories.
   - Sugar: Added sugars <10% of total daily calories.
   - Salt: <5g per day.

3. DIETARY HABITS
   - Cooking: Prefer boiling, pressure cooking, steaming, stir-frying. Avoid deep-frying.
   - Avoid: Ultra-processed foods, sugary drinks, alcohol.
   - Hydration: Adequate water intake is mandatory.

4. SPECIFIC FOR KERALA CONTEXT (DERIVED)
   - Reduction in white rice consumption is critical due to high glycemic load.
   - Replacement with whole grains, millets, or fiber-rich Kerala vegetables (snake gourd, bitter gourd) is recommended.
   - Coconut oil usage should be monitored due to saturated fat content (though traditional, limit quantity).
